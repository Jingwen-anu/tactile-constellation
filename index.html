The Idea

Touch, don't squint.

1

Choose your book

Pick one of three classic stories. Each has its own cast of characters waiting on the shelf.

2

Find the shapes

Every character is a tactile shape. A star for the hero, a triangle for the villain, a heart for love.

3

Place them on the board

The closer to the center, the closer to the main character's heart. Distance is meaning.

4

Connect with strings

Red for conflict. Green for friendship. Yellow for family. The relationships glow.

The Language of Shapes

Eight shapes for eight kinds of soul.

Inspired by Carl Jung's universal archetypes, these eight forms appear in every story ever told. Touch tells you who someone is before reading does.

Why this exists

For the 10% of children who don't read like everyone else.

Roughly one in ten children has dyslexia. For them, by the time they decode the third paragraph, the first character's name is gone. The relationships that give a story its soul stay invisible.

Tactile Constellation translates a book's character network into something you can hold. Built using natural language processing — every shape size, every position is data-driven from the book itself.

Touch what would otherwise be invisible. Feel a story instead of reading it.

Made for hands, hearts, and the in-between.
